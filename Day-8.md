## 🎯 Day 8 Objective

As part of my SOC Analyst L1 learning journey, this room introduced one of the most important security concepts:

> Security is not only the responsibility of the IT team — every user plays a role in protecting an organization.

Before learning about attacks, detection, and monitoring, I wanted to understand why security awareness is considered a critical defense layer in modern organizations.

---

### Who is this for?
Everyone! It's everyone's responsibility to be security-aware. No matter your role at your company, you could be a potential target for cybercriminals; especially given your access and knowledge inside your organization.
* Employees and end users
* SOC Analysts
* Security Teams
* System Administrators
* Anyone using digital accounts and devices

### What is Security Awareness?
<img width="710" height="710" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6655f7f2-0972-438a-9fb6-2672d23a380b" />

Security Awareness is the process of educating people about cyber threats, safe online behavior, and security best practices to reduce the risk of human error.
Based on a report from IBM, human errors were the main reason for 95% of successful cyber attacks. The study concluded that people are the main threat to the security of a business. Being more security-aware will significantly help mitigate potential threats and risks to your organization

### Where does this apply?

* Corporate environments
* Government organizations
* Educational institutions
* Healthcare systems
* Personal devices and online accounts

### When should it be learned?

As early as possible.

Security awareness should be learned before studying advanced cybersecurity topics because many successful attacks target people rather than technology.

### Why is it important?

Many cyber incidents begin with a simple mistake such as:

* Clicking a malicious link
* Reusing passwords
* Sharing sensitive information
* Falling for phishing emails

A single user action can impact an entire organization.

### How does it work?

Organizations reduce risk by:

* Training employees
* Teaching account security practices
* Explaining common attack methods
* Encouraging strong password habits
* Promoting safe online behavior

Security awareness helps users recognize threats before they become incidents.

### Task 5: Cyber Threat Actors
Cyber threat actors are individuals or groups of people who maliciously aim to take advantage of system security weaknesses to compromise and gain unauthorized access to victim data, computers, or networks.

<img width="1008" height="516" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/cb53ac43-8d8c-47b0-b4d1-37b1bfee2d28" />

The motivation of threat actors may vary and can be categorized into different groups:

* **Nation-state** cyber threat actors are geopolitically motivated.

* **Cybercriminals** are financially motivated.

* **Hacktivists** are ideologically motivated.

* **Terrorist groups** are motivated by ideological violence.

* **Thrill-seekers** are motivated by satisfaction.

* **Insider** threat actors are motivated by discontent.

## Critical Thinking

### Question

A company employee reports receiving an email asking them to verify their account credentials through a link. The employee clicked the link but did not enter any information.

As a SOC Analyst L1, what would be your next steps?

### Answer Concept

My investigation approach would be:

1. Collect the suspicious email.
2. Analyze the sender, links, and attachments.
3. Determine whether the link is malicious.
4. Check web proxy, DNS, or endpoint logs for related activity.
5. Verify whether any credentials were submitted.
6. Look for similar emails sent to other employees.
7. Escalate if evidence of phishing activity is confirmed.

The goal is to determine whether the event was a harmless click or the beginning of a larger phishing campaign.
